The 4 Cost Leaks

Where Indian companies are bleeding cloud cash — and why no one notices.

Cloud billing is designed to be opaque. AWS alone has 500+ pricing dimensions. These four categories account for 80% of recoverable waste in most Indian mid-market cloud environments.

💸
12%

Idle & Zombie Resources

Instances running 24/7 with under 5% CPU utilisation. Dev environments never turned off. Test databases running since the last sprint. Unattached EBS volumes and elastic IPs accumulating monthly charges for nothing.

Example: ₹1.2L/month on ₹10L spend
📦
9%

Over-Provisioned Instances

Your engineers provisioned m5.4xlarge "just to be safe" and it runs at 18% CPU. Rightsizing to m5.xlarge with auto-scaling delivers identical performance at 60% of the cost — but no one has the time to audit 200 instances.

Example: ₹90K/month on ₹10L spend
💾
6%

Storage & Snapshot Bloat

Snapshots that were "temporary" from 2022 still running. S3 buckets in expensive storage classes for data accessed once a year. RDS backups with 90-day retention when 30 days is policy. GP2 volumes that should be GP3.

Example: ₹60K/month on ₹10L spend
🎟️
5%

No Committed Use Discounts

Paying On-Demand rates for workloads that have run 24/7 for 18 months. AWS Reserved Instances save 40–72% vs On-Demand. Azure Reserved VM Instances save up to 72%. Most Indian companies never buy them because "it feels like a commitment."

Example: ₹50K/month on ₹10L spend

These four leak types account for 32% average waste on ₹10L/month cloud spend — that's ₹38.4L per year recoverable without changing a single line of application code.

FinOps FAQ

Questions from CTOs and Finance Heads before they start

How much does the free cloud cost audit actually cover?▾

The free 7-day audit covers a complete analysis of your last 90 days of billing data across all services — compute, storage, database, networking, and any managed services. We identify every idle resource, every rightsizing opportunity, every Reserved Instance gap, and every storage inefficiency. The deliverable is a written report with prioritised savings opportunities, estimated INR savings per action, and implementation effort level. You get this whether or not you engage TechBridge for ongoing services. The only requirement is read-only billing API access — no changes are made to your environment during the audit.

Does cloud cost optimisation affect application performance?▾

Done correctly, no. TechBridge rightsizing recommendations are validated against actual workload performance baselines — CPU, memory, network I/O, and disk throughput — not just theoretical capacity. We never recommend downsizing a production instance without 30 days of baseline data confirming the lower resource profile is sufficient. Idle resource cleanup follows a 14-day quarantine protocol — resources are tagged and stopped before deletion, giving your team time to recover anything that was incorrectly flagged. Performance regressions are our risk to manage, not yours.

How does the 20% savings guarantee work — what triggers the credit?▾

The guarantee is measured at Day 90 by comparing your average monthly cloud infrastructure cost over months 2 and 3 against your baseline (the 3-month average preceding engagement). If the reduction is less than 20%, TechBridge calculates the gap: (promised 20% reduction − actual reduction achieved) × monthly baseline spend = credit amount. That credit is applied to the following month's management fee. The guarantee applies to Growth and Enterprise managed services plans, not standalone one-time audits. It covers infrastructure costs (AWS/Azure/GCP bills) — not TechBridge management fees.

We already have AWS Cost Explorer / Azure Cost Management — isn't that enough?▾

AWS Cost Explorer and Azure Cost Management are visibility tools — they show you what you're spending, not what you should stop spending or how to reduce it. They don't tell you which instances are rightsiable without performance risk, which workloads qualify for Reserved Instances, or how to structure Savings Plans against your usage pattern. Most Indian companies have Cost Explorer access and still have 25–35% waste because the tools show data without driving decisions. TechBridge adds the analysis layer that turns billing data into an action list — and then executes the actions.

Can TechBridge do FinOps for multi-cloud environments (AWS + Azure + GCP)?▾

Yes — and multi-cloud environments typically have higher waste than single-cloud because there's no unified cost governance. TechBridge uses a unified FinOps platform that ingests billing data from AWS, Azure, GCP, and ESDS simultaneously, providing a single INR-denominated cost view across all providers. Multi-cloud environments on TechBridge Growth and Enterprise plans typically see 35–40% waste (vs 30–32% single cloud) — meaning the savings opportunity is proportionally larger. The management fee covers all four clouds with no per-cloud add-ons.
